27. Statistical Inference for Regression

27.1 Robust Standard Errors (HC3)

OLS standard errors are biased when heteroscedasticity is present. Robust standard error estimators (like HC3) adjust the covariance matrix to provide reliable confidence intervals and p-values without assuming constant noise variance.

28. Causal Inference with Regression

Suppose a researcher sees that organic farming correlates with lower yields. Does organic farming *cause* the drop, or is it due to mountainous terrain?

28.1 Causal DAGs

Draw a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) representing your assumptions. Before stating causal estimates, you must control for confounders and avoid controlling for mediators or colliders.

Confounders

Affect both treatment and target. Must control to block back-door paths.

Mediators

Sit on the causal path. Do not control if you want the total causal effect.

Colliders

Common effects. Do not control; conditioning on them creates false associations.

29. Advanced Regression Families

29.1 Mixed-Effects Models

Useful when observations are nested in groups (e.g. communities in districts). Fixed effects capture global trends, while random effects model group-specific intercepts using partial pooling: $y_{ij} = (\beta_0 + u_j) + \beta_1 x_{ij} + \epsilon_{ij}$.

29.2 Bayesian Regression

Treats coefficients as random variables. Combines prior distributions with data likelihood to calculate full posterior probability distributions for predictions and coefficients.

30. Uncertainty Quantification

30.1 Conformal Prediction

Conformal prediction provides a distribution-free method to construct prediction intervals with guaranteed coverage under exchangeability.

Split Conformal Procedure:

  1. Split training data into proper train and calibration splits.
  2. Train the model on the proper train split.
  3. Calculate absolute residuals on calibration set: $s_i = |y_i - \hat{y}_i|$.
  4. Find $(1-\alpha)(1+1/n_{\text{cal}})$ quantile threshold $q$.
  5. For new points, interval is: $\hat{y}_{\text{new}} \pm q$. Guaranteed to contain the true value $1-\alpha$ of the time.
