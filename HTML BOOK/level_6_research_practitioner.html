31. Research-Grade Model Comparison

To compare models scientifically, you must evaluate them on the exact same cross-validation folds. Record fold-level metrics and perform a paired statistical significance check:

  • Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test: Non-parametric check for paired differences.
  • Nadeau-Bengio Corrected t-test: Math correction accounting for training set overlap in cross-validation loops.

31.2 Ablation Studies

Ablation tests isolate the impact of pipeline components by dropping features or steps one at a time. E.g., comparing your final LightGBM model with and without log-transforming target costs to demonstrate the transformation's utility.

32. Reproducibility & Experiment Tracking

32.1 Model Cards

A Model Card details the target applications, dataset origins, global and subgroup metrics, and potential biases of a model, making it clear where the model is safe to deploy and where it is not.

33. Regression in Production

33.1 Data and Feature Drift

Production models drift when incoming inference queries differ from the training distribution (e.g., changes in district inflation). Monitor feature distributions using the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test and trigger retraining schedules when drift thresholds are breached.
